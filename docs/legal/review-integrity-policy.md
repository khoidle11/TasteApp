# Review Integrity Policy Scaffold

Status: Draft scaffold, requires attorney review before public use.

## Purpose

TasteApp's trust promise depends on Dish Reviews being useful, food-specific, and resistant to manipulation. This policy should guide user-facing rules, internal moderation, and future enforcement systems.

## Dish-First Review Rules

- Dish Reviews attach to RestaurantDishes, not restaurants as monoliths.
- Required Food Quality Signals for MVP are taste, value, portion, temperature, and ingredient quality.
- Service, ambiance, convenience, and popularity are Experience Factors and should not silently dominate default Dish Rankings.
- Reports are moderation signals, not automatic deletion.

## Anti-Manipulation Rules

Prohibited conduct should include:

- Fake reviews.
- Coordinated review attacks.
- Restaurant-owner or employee reviews presented as independent customer experiences.
- Paid, discounted, gifted, or incentivized reviews without required disclosure.
- Review suppression, retaliation, harassment, or threats.
- Reposting copied reviews or copied photos.
- Creating duplicate accounts to influence rankings.

## Restaurant Boundary

Claimed Restaurants may later correct factual business, Location, menu, or profile information. They must not control Dish Rankings, suppress Dish Reviews, or pay for ranking influence.

## Moderation And Enforcement

TasteApp may label, queue, slow, down-weight, hide, remove, or preserve content based on Review Integrity signals. Enforcement should be explainable enough for product operations without exposing internal abuse-detection details to manipulators.

## FTC And Consumer Review Notes

Final public rules should account for FTC guidance and rules on endorsements, testimonials, fake reviews, review suppression, and disclosure of material connections.

## Legal Review Notes

Counsel should review final consumer-review language, disclosure requirements, restaurant-owner restrictions, enforcement discretion, and appeal expectations before launch.
