# Terms Of Service Scaffold

Status: Draft scaffold, requires attorney review before public use.

## Purpose

The Terms of Service should define the contract between TasteApp and users. It should cover accounts, user-generated content, user content licenses, platform rules, moderation, intellectual property, disclaimers, liability limits, dispute terms, and policy updates.

## User Accounts

- Users are responsible for accurate account information and account security.
- Dish Reviews, Saved Items, Reports, Catalog Contributions, and Review Photos belong to a TasteApp User profile.
- TasteApp may suspend or restrict accounts that violate legal policies, manipulate reviews, infringe intellectual property, or abuse reporting/moderation systems.

## User-Generated Content

Users may submit Dish Reviews, Review Photos, Reports, Restaurants, Locations, Canonical Dishes, Menu Items, RestaurantDishes, profile content, and other Catalog Contributions.

Users should promise that:

- They own or have permission to submit their content.
- Their content does not infringe copyright, trademark, privacy, publicity, or other rights.
- Their Dish Reviews reflect their own honest experience.
- They will disclose incentives, relationships, or conflicts where required.
- They will not submit fake reviews, copied photos, misleading location data, or manipulated catalog data.

## Content License

TasteApp needs a user content license broad enough to operate the product. Final legal language should let TasteApp host, store, reproduce, display, resize, crop, moderate, remove, distribute, promote, and create technical derivatives of user-submitted content for TasteApp product, moderation, safety, search, ranking, analytics, and marketing surfaces.

The final terms should clarify:

- Users keep ownership of their content.
- TasteApp receives the license needed to run and improve the product.
- The license continues where needed for backups, legal compliance, moderation records, public content already shared, and ranking/review integrity.
- Deleted content may be removed from public display while retained where legally or operationally necessary.

## Product Boundaries

- TasteApp ranks RestaurantDishes, not restaurants as monoliths.
- Core Ranking Truth should not be sold to restaurants or hidden behind premium access.
- Claimed Restaurants may correct factual profile, menu, or Location information in future tools, but must not control Dish Rankings or suppress criticism.
- TasteApp may change rankings, labels, and moderation systems to protect product integrity.

## Moderation

TasteApp may remove, hide, label, down-rank, restrict, or preserve content for legal, safety, trust, or product-quality reasons. Reports are signals, not automatic decisions.

## Legal Review Notes

Counsel should decide final language for arbitration/class waiver, warranty disclaimers, liability limits, indemnity, governing law, account termination, paid features, and international availability.
