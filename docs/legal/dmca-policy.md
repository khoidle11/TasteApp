# DMCA Policy Scaffold

Status: Draft scaffold, requires attorney review before public use.

## Purpose

TasteApp expects user-submitted content, including Review Photos in Phase 2. The DMCA Policy should explain how copyright owners can send takedown notices, how users can submit counter-notices, and how TasteApp handles repeat infringers.

## Designated Agent

Before public launch with user-uploaded content, TasteApp should designate a DMCA agent by:

- Publishing the agent contact information on TasteApp's website.
- Registering the same information in the U.S. Copyright Office's online DMCA Designated Agent Directory.
- Keeping the information up to date.

Official reference: https://www.copyright.gov/dmca-directory/

## Takedown Notice Requirements

The public policy should ask copyright owners or authorized agents to provide:

- Signature of the copyright owner or authorized agent.
- Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed.
- Identification of the allegedly infringing material and enough information to locate it.
- Contact information for the complaining party.
- A good-faith statement that the use is not authorized.
- A statement that the notice is accurate and, under penalty of perjury, authorized.

## Response Workflow

TasteApp should:

- Log the notice and affected content.
- Remove or disable access expeditiously when a valid notice is received.
- Notify the uploading user where appropriate.
- Track counter-notice deadlines and outcomes.
- Preserve internal records for repeat-infringer and legal defense needs.

## Counter-Notice

The final policy should include a counter-notice process for users who believe content was removed by mistake or misidentification. Counsel should provide final language.

## Repeat Infringer Policy

TasteApp should maintain a repeat-infringer policy and reserve the right to suspend or terminate users who repeatedly submit infringing material.

## Legal Review Notes

Counsel should finalize DMCA notice, counter-notice, repeat-infringer, restoration, and record-retention language before Review Photos or other user-uploaded media launch.
