# Content Policy Scaffold

Status: Draft scaffold, requires attorney review before public use.

## Purpose

The Content Policy defines what users may submit to TasteApp and what TasteApp may remove, restrict, or review.

## Allowed Content

Users may submit:

- Honest Dish Reviews focused on food-specific qualities.
- Review Photos they own or have permission to upload.
- Accurate Restaurant, Location, Canonical Dish, Menu Item, and RestaurantDish contributions.
- Reports identifying incorrect, harmful, duplicate, infringing, or low-quality content.

## Prohibited Content

Users may not submit:

- Photos, text, menus, or other content copied without permission.
- Fake, paid, coerced, or undisclosed incentivized reviews.
- Restaurant-owner or employee reviews presented as independent customer reviews.
- Harassment, threats, hate, explicit content, doxxing, or private personal data.
- False claims about a Restaurant, Location, user, or Dish Review.
- Spam, bot activity, coordinated review attacks, or vote/review manipulation.
- Content that violates copyright, trademark, privacy, publicity, or other rights.

## Review Photos

Users should upload only photos they took or are authorized to submit. A photo being "of food" or "of a restaurant" does not by itself make it safe to use. TasteApp should store photo source, uploader, moderation state, and takedown state.

## Moderation

TasteApp may remove, hide, label, down-rank, restrict distribution, or preserve content for legal compliance, review integrity, user safety, or product quality.

Reports do not automatically remove content. They create moderation signals.

## Legal Review Notes

Counsel should review consumer-review language, content-removal discretion, appeal expectations, repeat-offender handling, and jurisdiction-specific platform obligations before launch.
